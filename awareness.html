Common Fraud Tactics

Learn to identify these scams before they harm you

HIGH RISK

Fake Customer Service Calls

Most reported fraud type

How it works:

Scammers impersonate Postman staff, claim issues with your shipment, and demand payment via UPI/online transfer.

Red Flags:

  • Caller asks for OTP, UPI PIN, or online payment
  • Pressure to pay immediately for "release" of shipment
  • Call from personal number (not official helpline)
  • Refusal to provide official complaint ID

Protect Yourself:

NEVER share OTP, UPI PIN, or make payments over phone. Immediately report incidents to the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930

Phishing Emails/SMS

Digital identity theft attempt

❌ "URGENT: Your shipment #AWB12345 is held. Click: bit.ly/fake-postman-link"

Legitimate Postman communications:

  • Will come from @postman.com domains only
  • Will address you by name (not generic "Dear Customer")
  • Will provide valid tracking links to our official site
  • Will never ask for passwords or financial details

Fake Websites/Apps

Digital impersonation scam

Scammers create fake websites that look identical to Postman's official site to steal login credentials and payment information.

✅ VERIFY BEFORE YOU TRUST:

Official URL: postman.com
SSL Padlock in address bar
Company details in footer
Contact information visible

Emergency Reporting

Immediate action required

  1. DO NOT share any information
  2. Take screenshot/call recording
  3. Note phone number/email
  4. Inform your bank if payment made
